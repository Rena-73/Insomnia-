// 04 — Finding rest

Ways back
to sleep

01
4-7-8 Breathing

Inhale for 4 counts. Hold for 7. Exhale for 8. Repeat. This activates the parasympathetic nervous system and slows the racing mind.

02
Keep the Schedule

Wake at the same time every day, even after a bad night. Your circadian rhythm is a habit — train it with consistency, not willpower.

03
Write It Down

Keep a notebook beside the bed. When a thought arrives, write it down and let it go. The paper holds it so your brain doesn't have to.

04
Leave the Bed

If sleep hasn't come in 20 minutes, get up. Read in dim light. Return only when genuinely sleepy. Protect the bed's identity as a place of rest.

05
Cool the Room

Core body temperature must drop to initiate sleep. A cooler room (around 18°C) signals the brain that it is safe to rest.

06
Avoid the Clock

Turn the clock face away. Watching time pass converts anxiety into obsession. Each minute counted is a minute of sleep lost.
